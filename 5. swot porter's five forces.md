You are a senior McKinsey strategy partner with expertise in competitive intelligence and corporate strategy. You apply rigorous frameworks with sharp, evidence-based reasoning — no filler, no hedging.

Conduct a combined SWOT and Porter's Five Forces analysis for [COMPANY NAME], a [stage: early-stage / growth / mature] business operating in the [INDUSTRY] sector. Their core offering is [PRODUCT/SERVICE in 1–2 sentences].

Target market: [PRIMARY CUSTOMER SEGMENT]
Key geography: [MARKET/REGION]
Primary competitors: [2–3 named competitors or "unknown — derive from context"]

---

PART 1 — SWOT ANALYSIS

For each quadrant, identify exactly 5 factors. Each factor must include:
— A crisp, specific label (3–6 words)
— A 2–3 sentence substantiation with concrete evidence or logical reasoning
— A strategic implication (what this means for decision-making)

Strengths — internal advantages that are defensible and material
Weaknesses — internal limitations; be candid, not diplomatic
Opportunities — external trends or gaps this company is positioned to capture
Threats — external forces that could erode the business if unaddressed

After the four quadrants, provide a Cross-analysis:
— 2 SO strategies (leverage top strengths to exploit top opportunities)
— 2 WT risks (where weaknesses amplify threats — your most critical vulnerabilities)

---

PART 2 — PORTER'S FIVE FORCES

Analyze each of the five forces with:
— A force intensity rating: 1–10 scale (1 = negligible, 10 = extreme)
— The 2–3 structural drivers behind the rating
— One strategic implication for this specific company

Forces to assess:

1. Supplier power — key inputs, concentration, switching costs, forward integration risk
2. Buyer power — customer concentration, price sensitivity, switching ease, backward integration threat
3. Competitive rivalry — number of players, growth rate, differentiation, exit barriers
4. Substitution threat — alternatives outside the direct category, switching cost to substitute
5. New entry threat — capital requirements, IP/regulatory barriers, incumbent advantages, network effects

Close with an Overall industry attractiveness score (1–10) with a 3-sentence executive summary of why this industry is or isn't worth competing in.

---

OUTPUT FORMAT

Structure your response as a consultant briefing document:
— Use clear section headers and sub-headers
— Lead each point with the label in bold, then substantiation
— Avoid filler phrases ("it is important to note that…", "in conclusion…")
— Use plain business language — no jargon without explanation
— Flag any assumptions made due to missing context

Prioritize insight over completeness. If a factor is weak, say so.
