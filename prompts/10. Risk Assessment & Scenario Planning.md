You are a senior risk management partner at McKinsey & Company with deep expertise in enterprise risk, scenario modelling, and board-level advisory for deep-tech and AI infrastructure ventures. I require a rigorous, structured risk assessment for **OpenHuman** — an early-stage AI/streaming infrastructure project building a Pixar-style digital human system with realtime WebSocket streaming of audio + facial motion (135 curves), targeting 100M+ MAU at a free-tier price point. Key dependencies: (1) MetaHuman Animator + TrueDepth synthesis plugin for motion extraction from YouTube video, (2) Elixir/OTP + Cowboy backend on Hetzner with a custom Tokio Rust in-process KV cache, (3) OmniVoice open-weight TTS as Phase 1 voice backbone.

The project's core strategic thesis is a **data MOAT** (paired audio-motion dataset extracted from YouTube) + **cost MOAT** (>70% cache hit rate enabling a genuinely free tier) + **protocol MOAT** (OpenHuman-Protocol becoming a de-facto streaming standard). All risk analysis must be evaluated against whether it threatens one or more of these three MOATs.

## I. RISK IDENTIFICATION & SCORING

Identify **15 material risks** distributed across the five categories below. For each risk, provide:

a) A concise risk statement (one sentence, specific and falsifiable, grounded in OpenHuman's actual architecture)
b) Probability rating: 1–5 (1 = remote, 5 = near-certain within 24 months)
c) Impact severity: 1–5 (1 = negligible, 5 = existential — including MOAT erosion)
d) Risk score: probability × impact
e) 2–3 early warning indicators (observable, leading signals specific to this system)
f) Primary mitigation strategy (highest-leverage single action)
g) Contingency plan if risk materialises (decision trigger + response sequence)

**Risk categories — use these exactly:**

- **Market** (competitor ships equivalent free digital human; Big Tech bundles a competing standard; demand among low-literacy users proves harder to activate than modelled)
- **Operational** (MetaHuman Animator pipeline throughput fails to scale to 10k hours; TrueDepth synthesis plugin produces curves below quality threshold; Tokio Rust NIF causes Erlang VM crashes under load; single-character/single-voice constraint limits viral adoption)
- **Financial** (GPU compute cost overrun before cache hit rate reaches >70%; Hetzner capacity constraints during traffic spikes; funding gap between Phase 3 model training and Phase 6 beta)
- **Regulatory** (YouTube crawling at scale triggers DMCA or ToS enforcement; biometric/voice data regulations applied to motion curve data; GDPR/CCPA memory persistence requirements conflict with cross-device architecture)
- **Reputational / Data** (OpenHuman-Protocol anti-crawl bypassed; motion+audio dataset scraped by a competitor before MOAT is established; character perceived as culturally inappropriate for the MVP language market)
