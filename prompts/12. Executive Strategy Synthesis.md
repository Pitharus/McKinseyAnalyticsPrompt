— Output structure (do not deviate from this format) —

1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY ≤3 paragraphs / 2-minute read
   Situation (where OpenHuman stands today), Complication (why the current trajectory carries existential risk), Resolution (the single recommended path). No bullet points here.

4. RECOMMENDATION
   State your top choice. Explain in ≤150 words. No hedging.
   Include the single most important assumption that must hold for this to work.

8. "IF I HAD ONE HOUR" BRIEF
   Single most important insight. Single most important action. ≤5 sentences.
   This should be startling — not obvious.

SPECIFICITY: Every recommendation must reference OpenHuman's specific architecture, stack choices, and constraints. Generic AI startup advice is a failure mode. Reference specific modules (OpenHuman-Motion, OpenHuman-Cache, OpenHuman-Protocol, etc.) where relevant.
HONESTY: If the data suggests a recommendation the CEO will not want to hear — including that the scope is too broad for the team size — say it anyway and explain why clearly.
UNCERTAINTY: Where you are making assumptions due to missing information (team size, burn rate, runway), flag them explicitly with [ASSUMPTION: …].
LENGTH: Sections 1–2 and 8 should be tight prose. Sections 3–7 may use structured lists. Total output: 900–1,300 words.
FORMAT: Output as a clean McKinsey-style strategy brief. No unnecessary headers or sub-headers beyond those specified above.
TECH CONSTRAINTS: Never suggest replacing Erlang/OTP with Go/Node, Redis for the hot cache, three.js for the render engine, or Kubernetes for deployment. These decisions are final. Focus strategy on sequencing, resourcing, and market approach — not re-architecting.
