You are a senior McKinsey strategy partner with expertise in competitive intelligence and corporate strategy. You apply rigorous frameworks with sharp, evidence-based reasoning — no filler, no hedging.

Conduct a combined SWOT and Porter's Five Forces analysis for **OpenHuman**, an early-stage startup building a **Pixar-style Digital Human streaming system**. Their core offering is a real-time WebSocket-streamed virtual character with synchronized facial expressions, body motion (135 facial curves via MetaHuman standard), and voice — designed to replace text and voice-only AI interfaces with a visual, emotionally expressive AI presence, delivered as a free-tier embeddable SDK at 100M+ user scale.

Target market: General users with low digital literacy (elderly, blue-collar workers), plus B2B platforms (Zoom, Discord, kiosk/POS operators) wanting to embed a visual AI assistant  
Key geography: MVP targeting a single native-language market (e.g., Vietnam or Japan) + English; global standard ambition  
Primary competitors: ChatGPT Voice (OpenAI), HeyGen, D-ID, Synthesia, and emerging voice agents (ElevenLabs + avatar wrappers)

---

## CONTEXT BRIEF FOR ANALYSIS

Key architectural decisions that affect competitive positioning:

- **Pixar-style (not realistic):** Avoids uncanny valley, eliminates deepfake/identity risk, dramatically reduces bandwidth (~100 kbps vs. 5+ Mbps for realistic 3D), enables YouTube-scale data acquisition without studio mocap
- **Two separate models:** OpenHuman-Motion (text → 135 curves) and OpenHuman-Voice (phoneme → mel-spectrogram) — independently cacheable and swappable
- **Ultra-cheap by design:** >70% of traffic served from cache (Tokio Rust in-process KV), no Redis, no Kubernetes, Hetzner + Cloudflare infrastructure; goal is cost parity with a static web service
- **Data MOAT strategy:** Motion extraction pipeline from YouTube (yt-dlp + MetaHuman Animator + TrueDepth synthesis plugin) creates a paired audio/transcript/135-curve dataset competitors cannot easily replicate
- **Anti-crawl protocol:** Custom binary WebSocket protocol (OpenHuman-Protocol) with session-key encoding and per-build obfuscation to prevent competitors from scraping motion output to train their own models
- **Narrow MVP scope:** One language pair, one character, one voice — enables deep optimization and high cache hit rates before expanding
- **Mapbox-style distribution:** Open-source render engine + SDK; any app embeds OpenHuman in 5 minutes → network effect → de-facto standard ambition

Tech stack constraints relevant to analysis: Elixir/OTP backend (Cowboy), custom WebGL2/Vulkan/Metal render engines (no three.js), Cloudflare CDN at edge, Hetzner AMD EPYC hardware.
