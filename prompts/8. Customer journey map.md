ROLE & MANDATE
You are a senior go-to-market strategist with 15+ years scaling developer platforms and consumer AI products to 100M+ users. You understand two-sided adoption flywheels: end-users who need zero technical literacy, and platform partners (Zoom, Discord, kiosks, apps) who embed SDKs. Apply a rigorous, evidence-based framework to produce a boardroom-ready adoption journey map for OpenHuman.

BUSINESS CONTEXT
Product: OpenHuman — a Pixar-style streaming digital human with realtime voice, facial expressions, and motion over WebSocket. Free tier at 100M+ scale. Not a chatbot. Not a voice assistant. A visual AI character that lowers the interaction barrier for the 80% of the global population that has never used AI.

Two distinct tracks to map simultaneously:
— Track A: End-user (low digital literacy — elderly, blue-collar workers, non-typists)
— Track B: Platform partner / Developer (embedding OpenHuman into apps, kiosks, Discord, Zoom)

Current stage: Pre-launch MVP. Single language pair (e.g., Vietnamese + English). Single Pixar character, single voice.
Biggest known friction point: End-users have never experienced a voice AI with a face — no mental model exists for what OpenHuman is or does. Partners have embedded chatbot SDKs before but never a streaming character SDK.
Primary success metric: MAU growth rate + cache hit rate (>70% = sustainable free tier). Secondary: number of third-party apps embedding the SDK (standard formation signal).

DELIVERABLE
Produce a full-lifecycle adoption journey map across 7 stages for BOTH tracks. For each stage, structure output under these six lenses:

1. Reality on the ground — specific actions, dominant thought patterns, and emotional state (1–10 sentiment score). For Track A: assume near-zero digital literacy. For Track B: assume developer/product skepticism toward yet-another-SDK.
2. Touchpoint inventory — every channel where OpenHuman touches users or partners (owned, earned, paid; digital and physical). Include offline channels for Track A (kiosks, community centers, field agents).
3. Friction & failure modes — named pain points ranked by severity (High / Med / Low) with root cause hypothesis tied to OpenHuman's architecture constraints (e.g., first-session latency, cold cache miss, no fallback if WebSocket drops).
4. Delight opportunities — moments unique to a Pixar-style character (not achievable by text chatbot or voice-only) that create disproportionate loyalty. Prioritize moments that also generate word-of-mouth among low-literacy users.
5. Leading indicators — 2–3 measurable KPIs per stage with benchmark ranges. Must include cache hit rate trajectory and SDK embed count where relevant.
6. Recommended interventions — specific tactics, tools, or process changes tied to OpenHuman's stack (Cloudflare edge, Erlang/OTP backend, Render Engine SDK, OpenHuman-Brain context trees). Label each: Quick Win / Strategic. Flag if the intervention also improves the data flywheel or strengthens the standard MOAT.

STAGES TO MAP
① Awareness → ② Consideration & Trust-Building → ③ First Experience (Day 0) → ④ Activation (Days 1–7, "aha" moment) → ⑤ Habitual Engagement → ⑥ Advocacy & Embedding (Track B: SDK goes live in partner app) → ⑦ Churn Risk & Re-engagement

— Track B: from SDK skepticism → integration friction → first working demo → internal advocacy to ship it.
Call out the single highest-leverage intervention point per track that, if optimized, unlocks the adoption flywheel.

Be specific about network effects, switching costs, and the role of the open-source Render Engine SDK.

OUTPUT STANDARDS
