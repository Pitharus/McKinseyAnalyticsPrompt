**Prepared by:** Senior Risk Management Partner, McKinsey & Company

**Subject:** OpenHuman — Pixar-Style Digital Human Streaming System (Phases 1–6)

---

## IV. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

> _Note: Executive Summary placed first per board-packet convention; full analysis follows._

OpenHuman's risk posture is **HIGH**, dominated by concentration in three brittle dependencies: a YouTube-derived dataset whose legal foundation is contestable, a MetaHuman Animator + TrueDepth-synthesis pipeline whose throughput is unproven at 10k-hour scale, and a cost model that requires >70% cache hit rate to sustain a free tier at 100M MAU. Of the three declared MOATs (data, cost, protocol), the **cost MOAT is the most urgent failure point** — every other MOAT depends on it: without sub-cent unit economics, the protocol never reaches embed-ubiquity, and the data MOAT becomes a stranded asset. The single strategic decision that most changes the risk profile is **decoupling Phase 1 dataset construction from YouTube-only sourcing** (adding licensed podcast archives, CC-BY video, and a paid creator pipeline) before Month 6. This converts the largest existential regulatory exposure into a manageable cost line and prevents a single DMCA enforcement event from invalidating 9 months of pipeline work.

**Three priority board actions:**

1. **Commission an independent legal opinion on YouTube ToS / DMCA exposure** for the OpenHuman-Crawler dataset — _Owner: General Counsel_
2. **Establish a hard 90-day throughput proof for OpenHuman-Animator** at 1,000-hour-equivalent batch on Hetzner before committing Phase 2 capital — _Owner: CTO_
3. **Pre-negotiate a Phase 2 custom-TTS fallback contract** in case OmniVoice licence terms change — _Owner: Head of Models / Head of Data_

## III. SCENARIO PLANNING

Four 18-month scenarios spanning Phases 1–6 of the OpenHuman roadmap (May 2026 → November 2027). Triggers anchored to specific milestones and modules.

### Scenario 1 — Upside Case ("Standard Locks In")

**Narrative.** The OpenHuman-Crawler hits 12k hours by Month 5 with <5% rework; OpenHuman-Animator throughput is solved by Month 4 via a parallelised UE5 batch farm on Hetzner. OpenHuman-Motion v1 trains cleanly in Month 7; OpenHuman-Voice Phase 1 (OmniVoice fine-tune) ships at MOS 4.2 by Month 8. The Render Engine + OpenHuman-Protocol public spec drops at Month 9, and a regional super-app (e.g., Southeast-Asian or Latin-American flagship) signs an embed partnership at Month 11 — before any hyperscaler announcement. Pre-seeded cache (10k canned utterances) plus traffic concentration in one app drives cache hit rate to 75% by Month 10. Closed beta launches Month 12 with 8k users; partner integration goes live Month 14.

**MOAT impact.**

- **Data MOAT:** _Strengthened._ Dataset reaches 15k hours by Month 12; competitor must replicate from scratch.
- **Cost MOAT:** _Strengthened._ 75% hit rate at Month 10 validates unit economics; per-user monthly cost <$0.005.
- **Protocol MOAT:** _Strongly strengthened._ First-mover embed in a flagship app creates a precedent; second partner closes by Month 16.

**Quarterly timeline.**

- _Q3 2026:_ Crawler scales, Animator throughput proof complete, Phase 2 dataset milestone hit.
- _Q4 2026:_ Motion + Voice models ship; Phase 4 streaming demo public.
- _Q1 2027:_ Render Engine SDK released; first partner LOI.
- _Q2 2027:_ Closed beta with partner-app traffic; cache hit rate >70%.
- _Q3 2027:_ Partner production integration; Series B fundraise on standard-formation narrative.

**Strategic response.**

1. **Lock the partner faster than is comfortable** (CEO + BD): trade favourable embed terms for exclusivity windows in adjacent verticals.
2. **Pre-publish the Protocol spec at a recognised standards body** (CTO): IETF or W3C draft submission to legitimise the format before competitors arrive.
3. **Aggressively expand OpenHuman-Crawler** (Head of Data): use the funding/momentum window to extend dataset to 30k hours, deepening the moat against late entrants.
4. **Begin Phase 2 custom TTS earlier** (Head of Models): with capital and confidence, replace OmniVoice ahead of schedule to remove the licence dependency.

---

### Scenario 2 — Base Case ("Survives, Doesn't Win Yet")

**Narrative.** OpenHuman-Crawler reaches 8k hours by Month 6, but ~20% requires rework due to TrueDepth-synthesis quality drift (O2 partially materialises). OpenHuman-Animator throughput is solved but at 1.4× modelled cost. Motion v1 trains by Month 8 with acceptable but unspectacular lip-sync metrics. OmniVoice Phase 1 is stable. Phase 4 streaming demo lands at Month 10, two months late. Render Engine ships web-only at Month 12 (iOS/Android slip to Month 15). No flagship partner signs by Month 13; closed beta launches with 5k self-selected users. Cache hit rate climbs to 65% by Month 13 — short of the 70% target — because traffic is too diverse without a partner-app concentration. Series A closes mid-Month 14 at modest valuation on dataset + early traction.

**MOAT impact.**

- **Data MOAT:** _Built but contestable._ 8k-hour dataset is real but not insurmountable.
- **Cost MOAT:** _Partially proven._ 65% hit rate is on-trajectory but unit economics still negative at scale.
- **Protocol MOAT:** _Not yet formed._ No third-party embeds means the protocol is a project, not a standard.

**Quarterly timeline.**

- _Q3 2026:_ Crawler scales with friction; rework cycle.
- _Q4 2026:_ Motion + Voice ship at acceptable quality; minor delays compound.
- _Q1 2027:_ Streaming demo public, partner conversations begin without closing.
- _Q2 2027:_ Render Engine web ships, mobile slips.
- _Q3 2027:_ Beta launches without flagship partner; Series A closes on data narrative.

**Strategic response.**

1. **Re-prioritise partner BD over feature breadth** (CEO): mobile native can wait; one named partner before Month 18 is the difference between "interesting" and "fundable at Series B."
2. **Audit cache strategy** (CTO + Head of Infra): determine whether 65% is a ceiling or a stage; if structural, redesign pre-compute strategy to lift it above 70%.
3. **Consolidate dataset quality** (Head of Data): use Q3 2027 to clean and re-extract problem clips rather than expand volume — quality compounds.
4. **Begin Phase 2 TTS planning** (Head of Models): even without urgency, OmniVoice dependency is a single point of failure; start scoping the custom replacement.

### Scenario 3 — Downside Case ("MOAT Slippage")

**Narrative.** OpenHuman-Animator throughput hits a wall at Month 5: UE5 batch crashes >5%, per-clip cost stays at $1.80, and the team can only push the dataset to 3,000 hours by Month 9. Simultaneously at Month 7, OmniVoice publishes an updated licence prohibiting commercial use in B2C avatar applications, forcing a panic Phase 2 custom-TTS push 6 months ahead of schedule — diverting Motion-team capacity. Cache hit rate stalls at 45% through Month 13 because the dataset is too thin to support broad pre-compute coverage and no partner concentrates traffic. Closed beta is delayed to Month 15. Burn rate runs 60% above plan; Series A is a difficult bridge round.

**MOAT impact.**

- **Data MOAT:** _Erodes._ 3k hours is well within range of an open-source competitor; the moat thesis weakens.
- **Cost MOAT:** _Fails to form._ 45% hit rate means the free-tier-at-scale thesis is unproven; investors discount accordingly.
- **Protocol MOAT:** _Indefinitely delayed._ No public Render Engine SDK ships before bridge round; no embed conversations.

**Quarterly timeline.**

- _Q3 2026:_ Animator throughput crisis declared; team time absorbed by infra.
- _Q4 2026:_ OmniVoice licence change; emergency TTS sprint begins.
- _Q1 2027:_ Cache hit rate stuck; burn investigation initiated.
- _Q2 2027:_ Bridge fundraise, scope reduction debate.
- _Q3 2027:_ Beta launches at reduced ambition; team morale becomes a risk in itself.

**Strategic response.**

1. **Declare the throughput crisis publicly inside the company at Month 5** (CTO): no quiet missed milestones. Either fix it in 60 days or formally descope the dataset target to 5k hours and rewrite the data-MOAT narrative.
2. **Negotiate an OmniVoice grandfather clause** (General Counsel + Head of Models) immediately on licence change — even paid — to buy 6 months for a managed transition rather than a panic.
3. **Cut Phase 5 mobile native** (CTO): web-only render engine is sufficient for beta; the savings extend runway by ~3 months.
4. **Reframe the investor narrative** (CEO) from "three MOATs" to "one MOAT plus speed" — most likely the protocol MOAT — and pursue a partner-led recovery in Phase 6.

### Scenario 4 — Black Swan ("Hyperscaler Pre-empts the Standard")

**Narrative.** At Month 6 (~ October 2026), Google announces "Gemini Live Avatar" — a free, embeddable animated character SDK with native integration in Google Meet, Workspace, and the Android system layer. Open-weights for the motion model are released under Apache 2.0 alongside the SDK to commoditise the layer. Within 8 weeks, Microsoft announces a Teams equivalent and OpenAI follows suit with a ChatGPT Voice extension. The protocol-standard window slams shut before OpenHuman has shipped its Render Engine.

**MOAT impact.**

- **Data MOAT:** _Devalued._ Google's release implies an internal dataset of comparable or larger scale; OpenHuman's dataset is no longer scarce.
- **Cost MOAT:** _Survives but reframed._ Cache + edge architecture is still defensible vs. hyperscaler unit economics, but the 100M-MAU thesis loses its standalone-product framing.
- **Protocol MOAT:** _Eliminated._ OpenHuman-Protocol cannot become a standard against a Google-default protocol embedded in Android.

**Quarterly timeline.**

- _Q3 2026:_ Google announcement; immediate strategic review triggered.
- _Q4 2026:_ Hyperscaler ecosystem consolidation; OpenHuman team makes a fork-in-the-road decision.
- _Q1 2027:_ Pivot direction declared (see strategic response options below).
- _Q2 2027:_ Reduced-scope beta or full pivot underway.
- _Q3 2027:_ New thesis tested in market.

**Strategic response.**

1. **Trigger a 30-day strategic review at the announcement** (CEO + Board): do not assume the original thesis is salvageable; force a decision between (a) pivot to _open alternative_ (the "Linux to Google's Windows"), (b) pivot to vertical specialisation (e.g., healthcare, education) where hyperscalers won't focus, or (c) wind down.
2. **Open-source the OpenHuman-Render Engine and Protocol** (CTO) immediately to maximise relevance — the closed strategy has no future once Google open-sources its layer; openness becomes the only differentiator.
3. **Pivot OpenHuman-Brain into the productised layer** (Head of Models): the LLM-tree-context-dispatcher + cache architecture is _not_ what Google released; it remains defensible and can ship as a standalone product on top of any avatar SDK including Google's.
4. **Preserve cash for the pivot** (CFO): freeze hiring, defer Phase 5–6 spend, ensure 18+ months of runway for the new thesis to be tested before re-fundraising.

## CLOSING NOTES

The most uncomfortable truth in this assessment is that **OpenHuman has stacked three correlated bets**: the data MOAT, cost MOAT, and protocol MOAT all assume the team executes a complex pipeline faster than well-funded incumbents notice the niche. Three out of the top five risks (R1, O1, M1) attack this same correlation. Risk management here is not primarily about reducing individual probabilities — it is about **decorrelating the bets**: alternative data sources, fallback voice models, partner-led traffic concentration, and an open-protocol posture that survives a hyperscaler entry.

The board's most important question is not "what is the probability of each risk?" but "**which risks share the same root cause, and which mitigation reduces several at once?**" By that test, the highest-leverage actions are: (1) diversifying dataset sources beyond YouTube (mitigates R1, partially M3, partially D3), (2) pre-seeding cache before beta (mitigates F1, partially F2, partially M2), and (3) shipping the Protocol spec publicly earlier than is comfortable (mitigates M1, partially D1, partially scenario-4 exposure).
