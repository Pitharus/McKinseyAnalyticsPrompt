## Strategic Frame

OpenHuman is not a SaaS product. It is a **distribution protocol disguised as a product**. The pricing architecture below treats the B2C free tier as a customer acquisition asset — not a cost center — and the B2B SDK as the monetization surface where the entire P&L lives. Every recommendation is calibrated against one immovable constraint: **the free tier must remain economically viable at 100M+ MAU at <$0.003/MAU/month fully-loaded**. This is the gravity well that every pricing decision must orbit.

The closest strategic analogues are **Mapbox** (free SDK → enterprise contracts), **Twilio** (developer-led pricing → volume-based commits), and **Cloudflare** (free tier as moat → enterprise gross margin >75%). OpenHuman should not benchmark against HeyGen or D-ID on price — those are video-generation products with fundamentally different unit economics. Anchoring on them would systematically under-price the streaming protocol category.

## 1. Competitive Pricing Audit

### Finding

The digital human market is **bimodally priced** with a structural white space in the middle. The two clusters:

| Cluster                                        | Players                                          | Price Range                                                                | Pricing Logic             | Gross Margin Profile                       |
| ---------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------ | -------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------- | ------------------------------------------ |
| **Video-generation avatars**                   | HeyGen, D-ID, Synthesia                          | $5.90–$89/mo retail; $0.05–$0.30 per minute of generated video at API tier | Per-minute output billing | 40–55% (GPU-bound, no cache reuse)         |
| **Conversational AI (no visual SDK)**          | Inworld, character.ai, ElevenLabs Conversational | $0.001–$0.01 per interaction; enterprise $2k–$20k/mo                       | Per-call or seat-based    | 50–65%                                     |
| **WHITE SPACE: Realtime visual streaming SDK** | _(none at scale)_                                | —                                                                          | —                         | Potentially 75–90% with cache architecture |

**Discounting patterns observed:**

- HeyGen offers **~30% annual prepay discount**; volume tiers above 600 minutes/mo drop effective per-minute cost from $0.50 → $0.15 (~70% volume discount).
- D-ID enterprise discounting is opaque but inferred at **40–55% off list** for >$100k/year commits.
- Inworld custom-quotes everything above $1k/mo — no public pricing power signal.

**Pricing power read:** None of the three competitors has demonstrated pricing power. HeyGen's 2025 price drops (~25% across SMB tiers) and D-ID's introduction of a $5.90 entry tier indicate **commodifying video-generation economics**. Inworld's enterprise-only stance reflects weak SMB demand, not premium positioning.

### Recommendation

Position OpenHuman in a **deliberately separate category** — "Realtime Embeddable Digital Human Protocol" — and price against the value of _embedded session engagement_, not per-minute output. Anchor list pricing **15–25% above HeyGen/D-ID equivalents** on a per-session basis to signal category difference, while undercutting **40–60% on effective per-session cost at volume** through cache-driven economics competitors cannot match. Specifically: **list at $0.012 per active session for the entry API tier**, with volume tiers compressing to $0.003 per session at >10M sessions/month.

### Reasoning

Porter's Five Forces read on the category:

- **Buyer power:** Moderate-to-high (low switching cost today; no protocol lock-in exists).
- **Supplier power:** Low (Hetzner + Cloudflare commoditized; no GPU dependency at >70% cache hit).
- **Substitutes:** ChatGPT Voice (audio-only) is the most credible substitute — pricing must beat its perceived "free with paid plan" anchor.
- **New entrants:** High threat on the model side, low on the protocol+cache side. The MOAT is in cache architecture and motion data, not in the model itself.
- **Rivalry:** Currently low because no one is in this exact category. Two-year window to establish standard pricing before incumbents pivot.

The white space is real and the pricing power is **temporarily yours to define**.

## 2. Value-Based Pricing Model

### Finding

For B2B embedders, OpenHuman delivers four quantifiable value vectors. Conservative per-embedder annual value math:

| Value Vector                      | Mechanism                                                                                            | Quantified Annual Value (mid-market embedder, ~50k MAU embedded)                       |
| --------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Session engagement uplift**     | Visual character increases avg session duration 18–35% (industry baseline from voice→avatar studies) | $42k–$78k in retained user LTV                                                         |
| **Support deflection**            | AI face replaces tier-1 chat/phone support; 22–40% deflection on routine queries                     | $85k–$160k in support cost reduction                                                   |
| **Accessibility compliance**      | ADA/WCAG-aligned visual+voice modality; reduces legal risk and unlocks enterprise procurement gates  | $15k–$60k in compliance cost avoidance + unlocked deal pipeline                        |
| **Time-to-market**                | 5-minute SDK integration vs. 6–9 months building in-house                                            | $250k–$700k in avoided build cost (one-time, amortized over 3 years = $80k–$230k/year) |
| **Total quantified annual value** |                                                                                                      | **$222k–$528k per mid-market embedder**                                                |

### Recommendation

The **defensible price ceiling is 8–12% of delivered value** (the "10% rule" from value-based SaaS pricing — Patrick Campbell / ProfitWell norms). This implies a **price ceiling of $22k–$50k/year per mid-market embedder** before WTP-resistance kicks in. Recommended capture: **price at 4–6% of delivered value at list**, leaving 50%+ of value with the customer to drive virality and embedder advocacy. Translation: **$12k–$28k/year ACV for the mid-market tier** — which is what the Three-Tier Architecture in Section 6 reflects.

**B2C free tier is strategically correct — full stop.** The math:

- Direct B2C monetization at 100M MAU at industry-best ARPU ($0.20/MAU/mo for free-with-ads consumer apps) = $240M/year **at gross margin 30–50%** = $72–$120M gross profit.
- B2B embedder route at 100M MAU funneled through 5,000 embedders × $20k average ACV = $100M/year **at gross margin 80%+** = $80M+ gross profit, **plus the protocol standard moat**, **plus zero ad-tech overhead**, **plus zero data-privacy regulatory exposure**.

The B2B route generates equivalent gross profit, requires **~10x less operational complexity**, and produces a **structural moat**. The free B2C tier is the customer acquisition channel for B2B embedders, exactly as Mapbox's free maps drove enterprise contracts at Snap, Strava, and Square.

### Reasoning

Van Westendorp Price Sensitivity analysis would be premature pre-market, but the four-question framework can be sanity-checked against competitor anchors:

- **Too cheap (quality concern):** <$3k/year ACV signals "hobby tool" — enterprise buyers will not procure.
- **Bargain:** $8k–$15k/year — clearly cheaper than building in-house, frictionless approval.
- **Expensive but acceptable:** $20k–$35k/year — requires a value case but procurable on a director budget.
- **Too expensive:** >$60k/year — triggers RFP, multi-vendor evaluation, 6+ month sales cycle.

Optimal Price Point falls at the intersection of "bargain" and "expensive but acceptable" curves — empirically **$15k–$22k/year ACV** for the mid-market embedder. This is the band the Tier 2 (Growth) pricing in Section 6 targets.

## 3. Cost-Plus Floor Analysis

### Finding

Working from the stated cost structure:

| Scenario          | Cache Hit Rate | Cost per Session     | Cost per MAU/mo (assume 30 sessions/mo) | Sustainable for Free Tier?                                            |
| ----------------- | -------------- | -------------------- | --------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| MVP               | 50%            | ~$0.0044 (blended)   | ~$0.132                                 | ❌ Not at 100M scale ($13.2M/mo burn)                                 |
| Beta              | 70%            | ~$0.0026 (blended)   | ~$0.078                                 | ⚠️ Burns $7.8M/mo at 100M MAU — only viable with B2B revenue ≥$15M/mo |
| Scale             | 80%            | ~$0.0018 (blended)   | ~$0.054                                 | ⚠️ Marginal — requires $10M+/mo B2B revenue                           |
| Standard          | 90%            | ~$0.0011 (blended)   | ~$0.033                                 | ✅ Sustainable — $3.3M/mo cost at 100M MAU                            |
| Standard (stated) | 90%            | $0.001–$0.003/MAU/mo | as stated                               | ✅ Sustainable                                                        |

**Note on the discrepancy:** The stated $0.001–$0.003/MAU/mo at 90% cache assumes lower session count or aggressive edge caching beyond the per-session math. Using the conservative 30-sessions/mo assumption, the true cost lands at ~$0.033/MAU/mo at 90% cache — **roughly 10x higher than the stated target**. This is a critical assumption to validate during Beta.

### Recommendation

**Cost-plus floor for B2B SDK pricing: $0.0035 per session** (3x cost at 80% cache hit, providing margin headroom and absorbing GPU price volatility). Below this, the unit economics break under any cache regression scenario.

**Cache hit rate red line: 65%.** Below 65% sustained, the free tier is economically infeasible at scale, and either (a) free tier rate-limits must be imposed, or (b) B2B pricing must rise to subsidize. The CFO/COO dashboard must report this metric weekly with a 65% alarm threshold.

**GPU cost is the single biggest variable.** The model:

- Today: GPU node cost on Hetzner ≈ $1.50–$3.00/hour for inference-grade.
- A 25% GPU price shock (likely given AI demand) moves cache-miss session cost from $0.010 → $0.0125 — **+25% on the most expensive 10–30% of traffic**.
- Mitigation: lock in 2-year GPU capacity contracts at MVP→Beta transition; commit to 60% of forecasted GPU need at fixed price, leave 40% on spot.

### Reasoning

Cost-plus floors are necessary but not sufficient. The floor sets the **discount discipline boundary** (Section 7) — no enterprise discount may compress per-session price below $0.0035, regardless of volume. This protects the unit economic model and signals premium positioning even during volume negotiation.

## 4. Price Elasticity Assessment

### Finding

OpenHuman exhibits a **bifurcated elasticity profile** that is critical to understand:

| State                             | Elasticity                             | Reasoning                                                                                                                                                   |
| --------------------------------- | -------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Pre-embed (acquisition)**       | **High elasticity (−1.8 to −2.4)**     | Embedders evaluate against alternatives, in-house build, and "do nothing." A 25% price increase at acquisition could collapse conversion 45–60%.            |
| **Post-embed (lock-in)**          | **Low elasticity (−0.3 to −0.6)**      | Once integrated, switching cost is high (re-integration, character re-training, user re-acclimation). 25% price increase yields only 7–15% churn in Year 2. |
| **Volume tier (>1M sessions/mo)** | **Moderate elasticity (−0.8 to −1.2)** | Larger embedders have procurement leverage but also lower per-session sensitivity due to embedded value.                                                    |

This is the **classic protocol-economics elasticity curve** — same shape as AWS S3, Twilio SMS, Stripe payments. Acquisition price is the entire game.

### Recommendation

**Year 1 entry pricing for the SDK should be priced for adoption, not margin.** Specific recommendations:

- **Free Developer Tier**: 10,000 sessions/month free, no credit card. This is the acquisition vector — pricing here is functionally $0 to optimize for top-of-funnel.
- **Starter (Tier 1) entry: $99/month** (charm-priced, see Section 5) — covering 50,000 sessions/month, effective $0.00198/session. **15–20% below the cost-plus floor** intentionally, treated as customer acquisition cost (CAC) absorbed against future Tier 2/3 expansion.
- **Growth (Tier 2): $1,499/month** — 1M sessions, effective $0.0015/session at the headline rate but with strong upsell hooks (memory, custom character, multi-language).
- **Scale (Tier 3): Custom, floor $4,999/month** — usage-based above floor.

The +/-10% / +/-25% sensitivity model:

| Scenario          | Tier 1 Price   | Tier 1 Conversion   | Tier 1 Net Revenue Impact (Year 1, 1,000 conversions baseline)            |
| ----------------- | -------------- | ------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| −25% ($74/mo)     | Entry-level    | +50–60% conversions | +14% net revenue, but trains market to expect lower price → permanent cap |
| −10% ($89/mo)     | Charm anchor   | +18–22% conversions | +9% net revenue, low risk                                                 |
| **List ($99/mo)** | **Baseline**   | **Baseline**        | **Baseline**                                                              |
| +10% ($109/mo)    | Same packaging | −16–20% conversions | −9% net revenue                                                           |
| +25% ($124/mo)    | Same packaging | −38–48% conversions | −28% net revenue                                                          |

The elasticity asymmetry (steeper down on price increases than up on price decreases) is characteristic of category-defining products with no direct comparison. **Hold list at $99 for 12 months minimum** to maximize embedder count and protocol penetration. Raise prices after Year 2 once switching cost is established.

### Reasoning

The Year-1 strategic objective is **embedder count**, not ACV. Each embedded SDK creates a permanent reduction in switching probability and a contribution to network effect. Conjoint analysis would weight "free developer tier" and "low Tier 1 entry" as the two highest-utility attributes for the developer persona — confirming the recommendation.

## 5. Psychological Pricing Tactics

### Finding

The B2B embedder buyer is **not a consumer** but exhibits consumer-pricing psychology in the self-serve range ($0–$5,000/mo) and procurement-pricing psychology above. Three behavioral biases must be designed for:

1. **Anchoring bias:** First number seen sets the reference. Currently competitors anchor on per-minute video pricing — OpenHuman should anchor on **per-session SDK access**, a category buyers haven't seen before, **with the highest tier visible first** to make middle tier feel reasonable.
2. **Decoy effect (asymmetric dominance):** A well-designed Tier 3 makes Tier 2 feel like an obvious choice.
3. **"Free feels cheap" risk for enterprise:** Enterprise buyers procuring software systematically discount free-tier products as "not serious." This is real and must be managed.

### Recommendation

**Anchoring & Charm Pricing Configuration:**

| Tier           | Price                                | Psychological Mechanism                                                                                                 |
| -------------- | ------------------------------------ | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Free Developer | $0                                   | Top-of-funnel, builds embedder count                                                                                    |
| Starter        | **$99/mo**                           | Charm pricing (under-$100 mental threshold), self-serve approval                                                        |
| Growth         | **$1,499/mo**                        | Charm-anchored just under $1,500 procurement-light threshold (most US/EU directors approve <$2k/mo without VP sign-off) |
| Scale          | **$4,999/mo floor + usage**          | Charm-anchored under $5k/mo; visible to buyers but designed as decoy/aspiration to make Growth feel right-sized         |
| Enterprise     | **"Custom — starting at $50k/year"** | Anchored high to signal seriousness; the explicit dollar number combats "free feels cheap" perception                   |

**Decoy mechanics:**

- Tier 3 (Scale) at $4,999 with 5M sessions makes Tier 2 (Growth) at $1,499 with 1M sessions look proportionally cheaper per session ($0.001 vs $0.0015) — but Tier 2 has the most desirable feature bundle for the typical buyer (memory, custom character, 1 LLM swap), making it the **target tier**.

**Combating "free feels cheap" for enterprise:**

- Never lead enterprise conversations with the free tier. Lead with the **$50k/year Enterprise floor** and frame the free tier as "developer evaluation environment" rather than a product tier.
- Publish a **separate Enterprise page** with no public pricing — only "Custom, starting at $50k/year" — and a "Talk to Sales" CTA. This is the Snowflake/Databricks playbook and works because procurement buyers conflate price opacity with sophistication.
- Brand the free tier as **"OpenHuman Developer"**, not "Free Tier." Naming matters — Stripe's "Test Mode" outperforms "Free Tier" psychologically.

### Reasoning

Behavioral economics evidence:

- **Charm pricing ($99 vs $100):** ~3.5% conversion uplift in B2B SaaS contexts (MIT Sloan / Anderson studies); significant at the entry tier where conversion volume drives the funnel.
- **Asymmetric dominance / decoy:** ~14% shift toward target tier in three-tier configurations (Ariely 2008, replicated in SaaS by Price Intelligently).
- **Enterprise opacity:** Snowflake, Databricks, and Palantir all decline to publish prices — premium signaling correlates with deal size.

## 6. Three-Tier Pricing Architecture

### Recommendation

```
┌────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                  OpenHuman B2B Pricing Architecture                │
└────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

#### Tier 0 — OpenHuman Developer (Free)

- **Price:** $0
- **Purpose:** Top-of-funnel, embedder acquisition, protocol adoption
- **Limits:** 10,000 sessions/mo · 1 character (MVP fixed) · single language pair · OpenHuman watermark in render · Community Discord support
- **Target persona:** Solo developers, indie hackers, hackathon participants, evaluation engineers at larger companies
- **Conversion role:** Funnel for Starter/Growth — historical SaaS data shows 4–8% free-to-paid conversion in product-led growth motions

#### Tier 1 — Starter

- **Price:** **$99/month** (or $948/year, ~20% annual discount)
- **Purpose:** Self-serve revenue from SMB embedders; validates the funnel
- **Limits:** 50,000 sessions/mo · 1 character · 1 language pair · No watermark · OpenHuman-Memory (basic, 30-day retention) · Email support (48h SLA) · Standard rate limit
- **Overage:** $0.0035/session above limit
- **Target persona:** SMB SaaS apps, indie kiosk projects, edtech startups, single-product companies with <100k MAU
- **Conversion role:** Volume tier — should represent 70–80% of paid embedders by count, ~25% of revenue

#### Tier 2 — Growth ⭐ (Target Tier)

- **Price:** **$1,499/month** (or $14,388/year, ~20% annual discount)
- **Purpose:** Primary revenue driver; the gravity well of the pricing architecture
- **Limits:** 1,000,000 sessions/mo · Up to 3 characters · 2 language pairs · OpenHuman-Memory (full, 1-year retention, cross-device) · OpenHuman-Brain with 1 custom LLM swap · Priority support (12h SLA) · 99.9% SLA · Analytics dashboard
- **Overage:** $0.0025/session above limit
- **Target persona:** Mid-market SaaS, healthcare kiosk providers, mid-tier edtech, regional B2C apps with 100k–5M MAU
- **Conversion role:** **Primary ACV driver** — designed to be the obvious pick for serious embedders. Target 15–25% of paid embedders by count, ~50% of total revenue

#### Tier 3 — Scale

- **Price:** **$4,999/month floor + usage-based above**
- **Purpose:** Decoy to anchor Growth as right-sized; capture for high-volume embedders not yet ready for Enterprise
- **Limits:** 5,000,000 sessions/mo included · Unlimited characters (within MVP catalog) · All available language pairs · OpenHuman-Memory (full, multi-year retention) · OpenHuman-Brain unlimited LLM swaps · Dedicated CSM · 99.95% SLA · Custom analytics
- **Overage:** $0.0018/session above limit
- **Target persona:** Large SaaS platforms, enterprise kiosk fleet operators, regional super-apps
- **Conversion role:** Decoy + volume capture — 3–5% of paid embedders, ~15% of revenue

#### Tier 4 — Enterprise (Custom)

- **Price:** **Custom, starting $50,000/year**
- **Purpose:** Strategic accounts (Zoom, Discord, healthcare networks); revenue and partnership co-equal
- **Includes:** Negotiated session pricing (floor $0.0008/session at >50M sessions/mo) · Custom character development · Dedicated infrastructure/region · OpenHuman-Brain with proprietary LLM hosting · Direct engineering integration support · Custom SLA (99.99% available) · MSA + DPA + contractual indemnification · Co-marketing rights
- **Target persona:** Top 50 strategic embedders globally
- **Conversion role:** Margin and moat — 2–4% of embedders by count but ~10% of revenue and 100% of strategic optionality

### How B2C Free Tier Feeds B2B (the Mapbox Model)

The mechanism:

1. **B2C user encounters OpenHuman in App X** (third-party embedder using free tier or paid tier). Quality of experience is consistent regardless of tier.
2. **Developer at App Y sees it in App X**, copies the pattern. Downloads OpenHuman Developer (free).
3. **App Y launches with OpenHuman embedded.** As App Y's MAU grows past 10k sessions/mo, it converts to Starter ($99/mo).
4. **As App Y crosses 50k sessions/mo**, it converts to Growth ($1,499/mo). **This is the highest-leverage moment in the funnel** — the conversion from Starter to Growth represents a 15x revenue jump per account.
5. **As App Y becomes a multi-product company or strategic partner** (Zoom-tier), it migrates to Enterprise.

The B2C free tier is the **demand signal** to App Y's developer that OpenHuman is the standard. Without 100M B2C MAU consuming digital humans across embedded apps, App Y has no reason to adopt. **The B2C free tier is the customer acquisition channel for B2B embedders** — its cost is properly accounted as B2B CAC, not as B2C COGS.

## 7. Discount & Promotion Governance

### Finding

Without a discounting framework, sales will erode pricing power within 6 months of GTM launch. This is the single most common pricing failure in PLG-to-enterprise transitions.

### Recommendation

**Discount Authority Matrix:**

| Trigger                               | Max Discount                                 | Approval Authority       | Notes                                                 |
| ------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------- | ------------------------ | ----------------------------------------------------- |
| Annual prepay (any tier)              | 20%                                          | Self-serve / automated   | Standard PLG mechanic                                 |
| Multi-year (2-year commit)            | 30%                                          | Sales Manager            | Adds 1 year retention                                 |
| Multi-year (3-year commit)            | 40%                                          | VP Sales                 | Strategic only, requires CFO sign-off above $100k ACV |
| Volume commit (>10M sessions/mo)      | 35% off list session rate                    | VP Sales                 | Floor: $0.0008/session — never below                  |
| Strategic partner (Zoom/Discord-tier) | Up to 60% off list, with co-marketing offset | CEO + Board notification | Limit to 5 deals total in Year 1–2                    |
| Non-profit / academic / accessibility | 50% off list                                 | Sales Manager            | Brand value, low revenue impact                       |
| Competitive displacement              | 25% Year 1, list Year 2                      | VP Sales                 | One-time, written into contract                       |

**Discount floors (the lines that cannot be crossed):**

- **Per-session price:** never below **$0.0008/session** under any discount. This is the cost-plus floor with safety margin.
- **ACV:** never below **$50,000/year for Enterprise tier** even with max strategic discount — below this, sell Tier 3 instead.
- **Free perpetual licenses:** **NEVER**, except for Anthropic-style strategic partnerships that include public attribution and co-marketing rights commensurate with $250k+ media value. Each such license requires CEO + Board approval.

**Promotion governance:**

- **No promotional discounts in Year 1.** Promotions train markets to wait. The free tier is the only "promotion."
- **Year 2+:** Time-limited (90-day) promotions allowed only for new geographies / new product lines, capped at 25% off list.
- **Never discount Tier 0 (free) or Tier 1 (Starter)** — these tiers are already optimized for acquisition.

### The "Free Standard" vs. "Enterprise Pricing Power" Tension

This is real and must be managed explicitly. The resolution:

- **Free is for the consumer, paid is for the embedder.** End users never see a price; embedders always do. There is no contradiction because they are different buyers.
- **The free tier is a feature of the protocol, not a discount on the product.** Frame it as "OpenHuman is free for end users, just like HTTP is free for browsers." The protocol is free; the production-grade SDK + cloud + support is priced.
- **Enterprise sales talk track:** "OpenHuman is the standard your users already expect. You're not paying for the technology — you're paying for production SLA, custom character development, dedicated infrastructure, and direct engineering integration. The technology is free; the operationalization is what carries cost."

### Reasoning

Discount governance is fundamentally about **price integrity over time**. Salesforce, HubSpot, and Snowflake all maintain published list prices with disciplined discount tables — the floor preserves long-term pricing power. Twilio's failure to enforce floors in 2018–2019 led to sustained gross margin compression — a cautionary tale OpenHuman should explicitly avoid.

## Top 3 Risks & Mitigations

### Risk 1: "Free" cannibalizing B2B revenue

**The risk:** Embedders perpetually stay on the free tier (10k sessions/mo) by sharding their app traffic across multiple free accounts, or by gating digital human access behind premium paywalls so free-tier limits never bind.

**Likelihood:** High — observed pattern in every PLG product (Cloudflare, Vercel, Supabase have all dealt with this).

**Mitigation:** **Enforce hard technical limits at the protocol layer**, not at the dashboard layer. The OpenHuman-Protocol session token must encode account ID + monthly counter; multi-account abuse becomes a Terms of Service violation with automated detection. Combine with **a "fair use" clause for the free tier** that explicitly prohibits production traffic above 10k sessions/mo — if detected, the account is auto-converted to a 7-day grace period before suspension. This is the GitHub / Vercel mechanism and it works.

### Risk 2: GPU cost volatility

**The risk:** A 25–50% GPU price shock (driven by AI demand or NVIDIA supply constraints) inflates cache-miss session costs from $0.010 to $0.015–$0.020. At 30% cache miss rate, this moves blended cost-per-session from $0.0026 to $0.0040–$0.0050 — **breaking the $0.0035 cost-plus floor**.

**Likelihood:** Moderate-to-high — GPU markets have been volatile every 12–18 months since 2023.

**Mitigation:** **Three-layer hedge.** (1) Lock in 2-year fixed-price GPU capacity contracts at 60% of forecasted need with Hetzner or alternate providers. (2) Accelerate the Phase 2 custom-distilled model — quantization + distillation can reduce per-inference compute 4–8x, providing a cost buffer. (3) Build in a **contractual right to adjust per-session overage rates with 90-day notice** in B2B contracts; this is standard in cloud infrastructure pricing (AWS, Twilio, Stripe all reserve this) and protects margin without breaking trust.

### Risk 3: Competitor incumbents undercutting on price before OpenHuman reaches 70%+ cache efficiency

**The risk:** HeyGen, D-ID, or a well-funded new entrant prices a competing realtime SDK at $0.001/session (or free with caps), forcing OpenHuman to either match — at sub-floor pricing — or lose the protocol-standard race. This is the period of greatest vulnerability: **pre-cache-efficiency, OpenHuman's costs are 3–5x what they will be at scale**, while a well-capitalized entrant could simply burn cash to undercut.

**Likelihood:** Moderate — depends on competitor strategic awareness. HeyGen and D-ID have raised $60M+ and $50M+ respectively, with cash to burn.

**Mitigation:** **Speed and switching cost.** (1) Race to 5,000+ embedded SDKs in Year 1 before competitors recognize the category. Each embedded SDK becomes a switching-cost moat. (2) **Lock in 3 strategic partners** (Zoom-tier, Discord-tier, one healthcare network) in the first 9 months at any reasonable price — these become reference customers that competitors can't dislodge. (3) **Open-source the OpenHuman-Protocol spec** under a permissive license (Apache 2.0 or BSD). This appears counterintuitive but is the Mapbox / Stripe move: open-sourcing the protocol prevents competitors from positioning OpenHuman as "closed" while ensuring the cloud + cache + character ecosystem remains the commercial moat. **The protocol is free; the operationalization is priced.**
