# OpenHuman — Go-To-Market Playbook

### Vietnamese Market Launch · Path to Global Standard

**Prepared for:** Founding Team · **Horizon:** T−60 → T+90 days · **Budget:** $50,000 USD · **Document version:** 1.0

---

## Executive Summary — One Page

**This is not a product launch. It is a standard-formation play.**

OpenHuman is competing for a category that does not yet exist: a free, open SDK for embeddable Pixar-style digital humans. The relevant historical analogues are not "another AI assistant" — they are **MP3 (audio standard), Mapbox GL (embeddable map standard), and WebRTC (realtime communication standard)**. Each won by being free, embeddable, and technically dominant on a single dimension competitors could not match. For OpenHuman, that dimension is **per-user cost** — driven by the cache MOAT.

**The strategic implication of this framing:** a typical CAC/LTV model is the wrong tool. We are not buying users; we are buying **integrations** (developer track) and **format adoption** (protocol track). End-user MAU is a downstream output of how many apps have embedded the SDK, not a directly purchased input. Every dollar of the $50K budget must be evaluated against one question: _Does this dollar increase the probability that OpenHuman-Protocol becomes the de-facto digital human streaming standard?_

**Three GTM theses for the next 16 weeks:**

1. **Win Vietnamese developers first, end-users second.** End-users with low digital literacy cannot be reached directly — they must be reached through Vietnamese apps that already serve them (Zalo, MoMo, VNeID, telco apps, kiosk vendors). The developer track is therefore the gating constraint. **~70% of GTM effort flows here.**

2. **The MOAT compounds in this order: Data → Cost → Format.** The 10K-hour YouTube motion dataset is shippable today. The cache hit rate >70% follows from narrow scope (single character, single language pair). Format MOAT is the LAST to solidify — it requires 5–10 third-party integrations and a published protocol RFC. We must defend the data and cost MOATs with secrecy/scope discipline while making the protocol maximally public.

3. **Vietnam is a launch market, not the destination.** Vietnam offers (a) a YouTube data corpus we can crawl legally and at scale, (b) a developer community accustomed to embedding open SDKs (Zalo Mini App ecosystem precedent), and (c) an underserved low-literacy population that justifies the "80% of the world" mission narrative globally. Success metric is not Vietnam MAU — it is whether Vietnam launch produces 3+ international platform conversations (Zoom, Discord, regional super-apps) by T+90.

**The single biggest risk is not technical.** It is that we burn 4 months perfecting the MVP for end-users while a well-funded competitor (Inworld, Soul Machines, ByteDance, or a stealth lab) publishes an open SDK first and captures the format MOAT. **Mitigation: ship the protocol RFC in week 2 of the 14-day sprint, even if the model is still imperfect.** Format precedes polish.

**Decision-readiness of this document:** Every recommendation below is tagged with which MOAT it serves (Data / Cost / Format), an explicit confidence interval, and an assumption that — if wrong — would change the recommendation. Read assumptions first; debate them before debating tactics.

## Section 1 — Launch Phasing & Milestones

The 16-week horizon decomposes into three phases. Each phase has a **single dominant question** that must be answered before advancing. Failing the go/no-go criterion does not mean abandoning the phase — it means re-scoping the next phase to address the gap.

### Phase A — Pre-Launch (T−60 to T−1, weeks 1–8)

**Dominant question:** _Is the technical foundation good enough that a developer integrating today would not regret it in 90 days?_

| --------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------ | ----------- | ----------------------------------------- |

| ASR WER <10% on Vietnamese (clean speech)                                   | ML Lead                  | Week 4      | >15% → launch English-only first          |

| Protocol decoder reference implementation (web) open-sourced                | Developer Relations Lead | Week 8      | Blocking                                  |

### Phase B — Launch Week (T−0 to T+7, week 9)

**Dominant question:** _Are we capturing developer attention faster than competitors can respond?_

**Dominant question:** _Are integrations producing enough end-user signal to justify the cost MOAT thesis?_

| Milestone                                                                          | Owner archetype          | Target week | Go/no-go threshold                                       |

## Section 2 — Channel Strategy

Channels are ranked by **expected ROI in standard-formation terms**, not in user-acquisition terms. The relevant proxy metrics are _cost per GitHub star_, _cost per qualified SDK integration conversation_, and _cost per end-user reached through partner activation_. CAC math is included where applicable but should not be the dominant lens.

### Channel ranking table

| Rank | Channel                                                             | Track                | Proxy CAC                            | Expected volume (90d)                 | Time-to-first-result | Resource req                | MOAT          | Standard vs growth |
| ---- | ------------------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------- | ------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------- | -------------------- | --------------------------- | ------------- | ------------------ |
| 1    | **GitHub + technical content (RFC, blog, video)**                   | Developer            | $5–15/star                           | 5K–10K stars                          | 2–4 weeks            | DevRel + Eng (50% time)     | Format        | **Standard**       |
| 2    | **Vietnamese super-app & fintech BD (Zalo, MoMo, VNPay)**           | Developer (B2B)      | $500–2K per integration conversation | 10–20 conversations, 3–5 integrations | 4–8 weeks            | Partnerships Lead full-time | Format + Data | **Standard**       |
| 3    | **Hacker News, Show HN, Product Hunt launch**                       | Developer            | $1–5/star equivalent                 | 2K–5K stars within 7 days             | <1 week              | Founder + DevRel            | Format        | **Standard**       |
| 4    | **Open dataset release (HuggingFace, Papers with Code)**            | Developer/Researcher | Free distribution                    | 1K–3K dataset downloads               | 2 weeks              | ML Lead                     | Data + Format | **Standard**       |
| 5    | **Vietnamese tech communities (VietAI, Daynhauhoc, dev FB groups)** | Developer            | $0.50–2/qualified contact            | 500–1500 contacts                     | 1–3 weeks            | Community Manager           | Format        | Mixed              |
| 6    | **Kiosk/POS hardware vendor partnerships (FPT, Viettel)**           | Developer (B2B)      | $1K–5K per integration               | 2–5 integrations                      | 8–12 weeks           | Partnerships Lead           | Format        | **Standard**       |
| 7    | **End-user word-of-mouth via partner apps**                         | End-user             | $0.05–0.20/MAU (downstream)          | Dependent on partner activation       | 8–16 weeks           | Partner-driven              | Cost          | Growth             |

> **Confidence:** medium-high on ranks 1–4, medium on ranks 5–6, low on rank 7 (downstream of partner activation, hard to forecast).

> **Channels deliberately omitted:** Paid Google/Facebook ads (wrong audience for low-literacy end users; wrong economics for developer track), influencer marketing (not credible for infrastructure/SDK products), and traditional PR firms (slow, expensive, low signal). If these channels were to be added later, they would only justify themselves once an integration has launched a consumer-facing app needing direct user acquisition — which is the _partner's_ problem to solve, not ours.

### Why GitHub leads the ranking

For an open-source standard play, a GitHub star is approximately the equivalent of an email list signup for a SaaS — it is the lightest-weight commitment signal that compounds. 5K stars in 90 days is the rough threshold at which a project crosses from "interesting curiosity" to "thing developers feel they should know about" in the AI/infra category. Below 1K stars, recruiting integration partners is dramatically harder; above 5K, it becomes inbound. Every dollar spent here also serves the format MOAT (the protocol is more credible when its repo is starred) and the data MOAT (researchers downloading the open dataset cite the repo).

### Why super-app BD is rank 2, not rank 1

Super-app integrations produce far more end-user reach per unit effort, but they are slower (weeks 4–8 to first signed integration) and require GitHub credibility as a precursor. Walking into Zalo's BD team with 200 GitHub stars is materially harder than walking in with 5,000. GitHub-first is therefore the correct sequencing, but super-app BD is the channel that actually moves the standard MOAT to lock-in.

## Section 3 — Messaging Framework

### Overarching value proposition

> **"80% of the world has never used AI. They can't type. They can't prompt. They need a face to talk to.
> OpenHuman is the free, open-source way for any app to give them one — in under 100 lines of code, at one-tenth the bandwidth of any alternative, with zero risk of deepfake misuse."**

This proposition does three things at once: (1) anchors the problem in the unmet 80%, which is the only narrative large enough to justify a free-tier-at-100M-MAU infrastructure investment; (2) leads with developer simplicity ("100 lines of code"), since developers are the gating audience; (3) preempts the two largest objections to AI characters (bandwidth cost, deepfake liability) in the same sentence.

### Persona-differentiated messaging

| Persona                                                    | Core message                                                                                                                                                           | Proof point 1                                                                                                                                                                                  | Proof point 2                                                                                                                                           |
| ---------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **App Developer (indie/SaaS)**                             | "Add a real character to your app in an afternoon, without hiring 3D artists or paying per-API-call AI fees."                                                          | Embed SDK is <2MB; reference integration ships in `<openhuman-character>` web component. Free tier is permanently free — no usage cliff.                                                       | Pixar style avoids the uncanny valley that has killed every realistic-avatar startup since 2019; users perceive it as a _character_, not a fake person. |
| **Enterprise / Kiosk Integrator**                          | "Deploy a multilingual AI assistant on your kiosks/in-app surfaces with no biometric data, no deepfake liability, and 100× lower bandwidth than realistic 3D avatars." | No real human face = no GDPR/CCPA biometric scope, no PDPL (Vietnam) facial-data triggers, no deepfake legal exposure.                                                                         | ~100 kbps motion+audio stream vs. >5 Mbps for realistic 3D — works on 3G in rural Vietnam, on shared kiosk wifi, on low-end Android.                    |
| **Low-literacy End User** _(reached through partner apps)_ | _"Bạn có thể nói chuyện như bạn đang nói với một người thật."_ (You can talk like you're talking to a real person.)                                                    | Partner app surfaces character with first-time user tutorial: tap once → character greets in Vietnamese → user replies in voice → character responds with face + voice. No keyboard, no menus. | Character is consistent across all apps the user opens (cross-device memory) — they recognize the same "person" everywhere, lowering re-learning cost.  |

> **Frame discipline:** Every external message should reference _at least one_ of the three Pixar rationales (uncanny-valley avoidance / no deepfake risk / 100× bandwidth). These are the differentiators that actually defend the category. Generic AI-character marketing copy would put us in the same bucket as Inworld/Soul Machines and lose the framing battle.

### What NOT to say

Avoid framing OpenHuman as "ChatGPT with a face" — this collapses the category back into chatbot competition where OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google have insurmountable advantages. The product is _infrastructure_, not an assistant. The correct framing is "Mapbox for digital humans" — a SDK that powers other people's products.

## Section 6 — Budget Allocation Model

For a $50K bootstrapped budget, the allocation principle is: **maximize spend on irreversible standard-building investments, minimize spend on reversible growth tactics**. A GitHub repo with 5K stars is irreversible; a paid ad campaign is not.

### Base allocation ($50,000)

| Category                                                                                                                                          | $       | %   | Rationale                                                                                                  | Standard vs growth |
| ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------- | --- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------ |
| **Developer Relations & content** (technical writer contractor, video production for demos, conference travel for 1 Vietnamese AI/dev conference) | $14,000 | 28% | Highest-leverage category. Every dollar produces compounding GitHub credibility.                           | Standard           |
| **Partnership BD support** (travel, demo equipment, partnership pilot infrastructure, 1 contractor BD assistant)                                  | $11,000 | 22% | Super-app and kiosk BD is the only path to end-user reach. Underfunding here = no end-user signal at T+90. | Standard           |
| **Open dataset release & research** (annotation cleanup, hosting on HuggingFace, arXiv preprint editing)                                          | $6,000  | 12% | Cheapest way to attract ML-researcher mindshare. Researchers cite → credibility compounds.                 | Standard           |
| **Community management** (part-time community manager Vietnamese-fluent, Discord/Zalo moderation, swag for top contributors)                      | $7,500  | 15% | Without active community, GitHub stars don't convert to integrations.                                      | Mixed              |
| **Launch week amplification** (HN/Product Hunt support, Vietnamese tech press outreach, launch-day video assets)                                  | $4,000  | 8%  | Concentrated burst, single use. Caps the upside at "good launch", doesn't compound.                        | Mixed              |
| **Contingency / paid amplification reserve** (deploy only if launch underperforms benchmarks)                                                     | $5,500  | 11% | Reserve for triggered scenarios (see Risk Register).                                                       | Growth             |
| **Legal & compliance** (open-source license review, Vietnamese data-localization legal opinion, partnership template contracts)                   | $2,000  | 4%  | Small but blocking. Without clean licenses + a clean PDPL opinion, super-app partners cannot sign.         | Standard           |

### Sensitivity table

| Scenario              | Budget  | Key changes vs base                                                                                                                                                           |
| --------------------- | ------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Constrained — 70%** | $35,000 | Cut contingency reserve to $0; cut launch amplification to $2K; cut DevRel content to $10K (defer 1 conference). Partnerships and dataset stay protected.                     |
| **Base — 100%**       | $50,000 | As above.                                                                                                                                                                     |
| **Expanded — 130%**   | $65,000 | Add $7.5K for second BD contractor (parallelize super-app + telco outreach); add $5K for second open dataset release at T+60; add $2.5K to extend community management hours. |

> **Allocation principle confirmed:** ~78% of budget ($39K of $50K base) flows to standard-building (DevRel, partnerships, dataset, legal, community). Only ~22% flows to amplification or contingency. **If this ratio drops below 70% standard-building in any revision, the GTM has drifted toward typical-product-launch math and must be re-anchored.**

> **Risks 3 and 6 are the existential risks** — both directly threaten the Format MOAT. The 14-day sprint (Section 9) is specifically designed to inoculate against risk 3 by establishing protocol primacy publicly before any competitor can. Risk 6 cannot be inoculated against pre-launch — it requires ongoing governance discipline.

## Section 9 — 14-Day Quick-Win Sprint

Three tactics, executed in days 1–14, designed to _simultaneously_ generate user/developer signal AND build standard credibility. These are the "must-ship" items even if every other GTM activity slips.

### Sprint tactic 1 — Protocol RFC v1.0 + Reference Web Decoder (days 1–7)

|                    |                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Action**         | Publish OpenHuman-Protocol RFC v1.0 on GitHub with full binary frame format, delta motion codec spec, and anti-crawl wrapper rationale (anti-crawl design discussed without compromising the rotation mechanism). Open GitHub Discussions for community feedback. Ship a reference web decoder (~500 LoC) under MIT license. |
| **Owner**          | Developer Relations Lead (writing) + Client Lead (decoder)                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   |
| **Resource cost**  | ~80 engineering hours over 7 days; ~$0 cash cost                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             |
| **Success metric** | RFC repo earns ≥500 GitHub stars and ≥20 substantive Discussion threads within 14 days                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       |
| **MOAT served**    | **Format (primary)** — this is the single most important standard-formation action of the entire 90 days. Inoculates against competitor risk #3.                                                                                                                                                                             |

### Sprint tactic 2 — Live Demo Embedded in 1 Well-Known Vietnamese App (days 1–10)

|                    |                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      |
| ------------------ | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Action**         | Land one design-partner integration that goes live publicly during launch week. Most likely candidates given pre-launch BD: a Vietnamese language-learning app, a customer-service portal of a mid-tier bank/telco, or a Zalo Mini App from a friendly developer. Even a small integration (e.g., a single FAQ-style character on an app's help page) is sufficient. |
| **Owner**          | Partnerships Lead                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    |
| **Resource cost**  | ~40 BD/integration-support hours; ~$1,500 from partnership BD budget for travel + co-marketing                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       |
| **Success metric** | Public Vietnamese app demonstrably running OpenHuman SDK by day 10, with shareable URL or screenshots for launch                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     |
| **MOAT served**    | **Format + Cost** — a real-world integration is the proof that the protocol works AND that the cost economics hold. Without this, the launch is just a tech demo.                                                                                                                                                                                                    |

### Sprint tactic 3 — Open Dataset Release: 1,000-Hour Vietnamese Motion Subset (days 5–14)

|                    |                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     |
| ------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Action**         | Release 1,000 hours of paired (audio, transcript, 135-curve) data on HuggingFace under a research-permissive license. Include a short technical report (8 pages) describing extraction pipeline. Submit to HuggingFace daily papers + Vietnamese AI research lists. |
| **Owner**          | ML Lead + Data Engineer                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             |
| **Resource cost**  | ~50 engineering hours for cleanup + cards; ~$2,000 from open-dataset budget for hosting + editing                                                                                                                                                                   |
| **Success metric** | ≥500 dataset downloads in first 14 days; ≥3 academic/research mentions                                                                                                                                                                                              |
| **MOAT served**    | **Data + Format** — counterintuitively, releasing 10% of the dataset _strengthens_ the data MOAT by making OpenHuman the canonical reference dataset for digital human research. Researchers cite our dataset, which means they cite our protocol.                  |

### Why these three, not three others

Each tactic produces an _artifact_ that persists beyond launch week: the RFC, the live integration, the dataset. Artifacts compound; activities (e.g., a launch-day press tour) do not. The sprint deliberately omits any activity that produces "a moment" without a persistent artifact.

> **Single-paragraph litmus test for the sprint:** If a competitor launches a similar open SDK 30 days after our launch, do they face a higher cost to displace us than they would have without these three tactics? RFC primacy → yes (protocol citation precedence). Live integration → yes (existence proof of partnerships). Open dataset → yes (research community alignment). All three pass.

## Closing — How to use this document

This playbook is an instrument for decision-making, not a script for execution. Three principles for using it well:

1. **Debate the assumptions before debating the tactics.** Every section flags assumptions ("Vietnamese ASR <10% WER", "super-app willing to embed", "10K-hour dataset achievable"). If any of these flips, the corresponding section is invalidated and must be re-derived. Tactical disagreements that ignore assumption disagreements waste time.

2. **Treat the gates as real.** A gate that says "without this, defer launch 30 days" must mean it. Open-source standard plays die from premature launches more often than from late ones — the format MOAT requires that v1.0 be defendable, because every developer who tries v1.0 and finds it broken is a developer who will not return for v1.1.

3. **The dominant question of GTM is not "did we launch well" — it is "did the protocol enter the consideration set."** At T+90, evaluate against the standard-formation framing first, the user-acquisition framing second. If 8K stars + 12 integrations + 1 international conversation → the standard play is on track regardless of MAU. If 100K MAU + 0 integrations + 0 international conversation → the standard play has failed regardless of MAU.
