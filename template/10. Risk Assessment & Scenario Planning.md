You are a senior risk management partner at McKinsey & Company with deep expertise in enterprise risk, scenario modelling, and board-level advisory. I require a rigorous, structured risk assessment for [BUSINESS / PROJECT NAME] — a [STAGE: early-stage / growth / mature] [INDUSTRY] business with key dependencies on [2–3 CRITICAL DEPENDENCIES: e.g. single supplier, regulatory approval, tech platform].

---

I. RISK IDENTIFICATION & SCORING

Identify 15 material risks distributed across the five categories below. For each risk, provide:

a) A concise risk statement (one sentence, specific and falsifiable)
b) Probability rating: 1–5 (1 = remote, 5 = near-certain within 24 months)
c) Impact severity: 1–5 (1 = negligible, 5 = existential)
d) Risk score: probability × impact
e) 2–3 early warning indicators (observable, leading signals)
f) Primary mitigation strategy (the one highest-leverage action)
g) Contingency plan if risk materialises (decision trigger + response sequence)

Risk categories:

- Market (demand disruption, competitive intensity, pricing pressure)
- Operational (supply chain concentration, talent attrition, technology failure)
- Financial (cash runway, FX exposure, funding gap)
- Regulatory (compliance burden, policy shift, legal exposure)
- Reputational (PR crisis, customer trust, data or ESG incidents)

---

II. PRIORITISED RISK MATRIX

Present a 5×5 probability–impact heat map. Cluster risks into four zones: Critical (score ≥ 16), High (10–15), Moderate (5–9), and Low (<5). Surface the top 5 risks requiring immediate leadership attention with one-line rationale for each.

---

III. SCENARIO PLANNING

Model four forward-looking scenarios over a [HORIZON: e.g. 18-month] period. For each scenario, specify:

- Narrative: The coherent chain of events that leads to this outcome
- Revenue impact: Estimated % change vs base case
- Timeline: Trigger point and how it unfolds quarter by quarter
- Strategic response: The 3–4 priority actions leadership must execute

Scenarios:

1. Upside Case — conditions align, key risks do not materialise
2. Base Case — most probable outcome given current trajectory
3. Downside Case — two or more high-priority risks materialise simultaneously
4. Black Swan — a low-probability, high-consequence event that invalidates core assumptions

---

IV. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Open with a 150-word executive summary suitable for a board packet: overall risk posture (low / moderate / high / critical), the single most urgent risk, and the one strategic decision that most changes the risk profile. Close with three recommended board-level actions in priority order.

Format the full output as a structured executive risk report. Use clear section headers, concise prose, and a consistent table format for risk entries. Avoid filler language — every sentence should carry decision-relevant insight.
