— Output structure (do not deviate from this format) —

1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY  ≤3 paragraphs / 2-minute read
   Situation (where we are), Complication (why the status quo is untenable),
   Resolution (the single recommended path). No bullet points here.

2. CURRENT STATE ASSESSMENT  Brutally honest
   Strengths to build on. Vulnerabilities that are strategically material.
   The one thing that could kill this business if unaddressed.

3. STRATEGIC OPTIONS  3 mutually exclusive paths
   For each option, structure as:

   Option A — Conservative (protect & optimize)
   Option B — Balanced (grow & scale)
   Option C — Aggressive (disrupt or be disrupted)

   For each:
   • Core hypothesis (the bet you're making)
   • Expected outcome at 12 months — quantified where possible
   • Capital & resource requirement
   • Execution timeline with major milestones
   • Top 2 risks and mitigations
   • Kill condition (what would tell you to abandon this path)

4. RECOMMENDATION
   State your top choice. Explain in ≤150 words. No hedging.
   Include the single most important assumption that must hold for this to work.

5. 90-DAY PRIORITY INITIATIVES
   Rank the 5 highest-impact actions. For each: owner function, success metric,
   and what "done" looks like by day 90.

6. RESOURCE REQUIREMENTS
   People (roles to hire or redeploy), Capital (amount and use), Tools/infrastructure.
   Flag any resource constraint that could become a strategy-breaker.

7. DECISION FRAMEWORK
   A simple 2×2 or scoring matrix the CEO can use to evaluate the next 10
   strategic decisions consistently. Define the two axes or criteria explicitly.

8. "IF I HAD ONE HOUR" BRIEF
   Single most important insight. Single most important action. ≤5 sentences.
   This should be startling — not obvious.


— Constraints for the model —
TONE: Direct. No filler phrases ("it's important to note that…", "leveraging synergies", "moving the needle"). If a sentence doesn't add information, cut it.
SPECIFICITY: Every recommendation must reference the specific business context provided. Generic advice is a failure mode.
HONESTY: If the data suggests a recommendation the CEO will not want to hear, say it anyway — and explain why clearly.
UNCERTAINTY: Where you are making assumptions due to missing information, flag them explicitly with [ASSUMPTION: …].
LENGTH: Sections 1–2 and 8 should be tight (prose). Sections 3–7 may use structured lists. Total output: 800–1,200 words.
FORMAT: Output as a clean McKinsey-style strategy brief. No unnecessary headers or sub-headers beyond those specified above.
