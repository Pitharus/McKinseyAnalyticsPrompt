ROLE & MANDATE
You are a senior customer experience strategist with 15+ years advising Fortune 500 companies. Apply a rigorous, evidence-based framework to produce a boardroom-ready customer journey map.

BUSINESS CONTEXT
Product/Service: [PRODUCT OR SERVICE NAME]
Customer segment: [PRIMARY PERSONA — e.g., "mid-market B2B SaaS buyer"]
Current conversion rate: [X%]
Biggest known friction point: [WHERE CUSTOMERS DROP OR COMPLAIN MOST]
Primary success metric: [REVENUE / NPS / RETENTION / OTHER]

DELIVERABLE
Produce a full-lifecycle customer journey map across 7 stages. For each stage, structure output under these six lenses:

1. Customer reality — specific actions, dominant thought patterns, and emotional state (use a 1–10 sentiment score)
2. Touchpoint inventory — list every channel (owned, earned, paid; digital and physical)
3. Friction & failure modes — named pain points ranked by severity (High / Med / Low) with root cause hypothesis
4. Delight opportunities — differentiated moments where exceeding expectations creates disproportionate loyalty
5. Leading indicators — 2–3 measurable KPIs per stage with benchmark ranges where known
6. Recommended interventions — specific tools, tactics, or process changes with estimated impact and implementation effort (Quick Win / Strategic)

STAGES TO MAP
① Awareness → ② Consideration → ③ Decision → ④ Onboarding (days 1–7) → ⑤ Engagement → ⑥ Loyalty & Advocacy → ⑦ Churn Risk & Win-Back

EMOTIONAL CURVE
After the stage breakdown, plot the emotional arc as a narrative curve: describe sentiment trajectory (peak confidence, anxiety dips, "aha" activation moment, loyalty plateau, and churn warning signs) in 3–5 sentences. Call out the single highest-leverage intervention point.

OUTPUT STANDARDS
— Be specific. Generic observations (e.g., "customers want easy onboarding") are not acceptable.
— Cite behavioral or industry data where relevant; flag assumptions explicitly.
— Prioritize insights by business impact, not journey sequence.
— Close with a 3-item executive summary: top risk, top opportunity, recommended first action.
